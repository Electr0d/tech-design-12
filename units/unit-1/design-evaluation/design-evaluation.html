1.9 - Design Evaluation
Prototype

The prototype design unfortunately failed to meet many of the requirements set, mainly due to the base material used, which is bristol paper, instead of rubber. Bristol paper was used because the school does not have silicon 3D printers. The prototype does not have a sleek design because trimming bristol paper beyond a certain point will cause it to break in half because of how weak it is. Which meant I could not make the design any sleeker. The case does have a rounded design, easily holds on the user’s finger(s), comfortable, but is not durable, due to the thin rigid material. The prototype is very cheap to mass produce, costing around $0.5 to produce, is available in multiple colours, and does not slip from the user’s hands, but does not protect the front glass very well, and the tab is inline with the case, it does not stick out.
