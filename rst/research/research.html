Looking Back at my Previous Attempt

This is not my first time trying to redesign my room. About a year ago, I wanted to design my room in Blender, but my attempt fell short since I was a novice at the time. Here it is

A simple design of my room, in solid view.
A simple design of my room, in solid view.
A simple design of my room, in material preview mode.

The reason why is that it was taking me too long to model the bed and the chair and I didn’t know at the time how to design nondestructively. Nondestructive modelling means modelling something that can be edited later on easily. An example of a destructive modelling is the bed.

This is an example of destructive modelling. Changing the curve of the bed frame is too hard since there are too many vertices.

Since the problem is so bad, I have to remodel the entire bed frame, hence its called “destructive modelling”. Fixing this can be done by adding a cuboid, adding a loop cut in the middle, adding a bevel modifier, and moving the center vertices up to create a slope. Now changing the slope would be much easier since I just have to drag the two vertices in the middle up or down and make small adjustments to the bevel modifier.

The bed slope and can easily remodelled like this. The nice thing about this is that I can easily change the bevel amount and segments.
